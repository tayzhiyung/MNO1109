Mass Density
Mass density ρ is defined as the amount of mass M contained per unit volume V.

\rho=\frac{M}{V}

Unit for mass density is kg/m3

×
×

Specific Gravity
Specific gravity SG is the ratio of mass density ρ to a reference mass density ρ0, usually taken as the mass density of water, i.e. 1000kg/m3.

SG=\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}

Mass
The mass of an object is:
i. A fundamental property of the object
ii. A numerical measure of its inertia
iii. A fundamental measure of the amount of matter in the object.

The SI unit for mass is kg and can be derived by taking

M=V \times \rho

The mass of an object will not change.

Travelling Ship in Water of Different Mass Density
When a ship travels from waters of different mass density, its displacement volume, and hence its draft will change.

Remember that mass is always fix so,

M_{new}=M_{old}

and therefore

(\rho \times V)_{new}=(\rho \times V)_{old}

by further simplify the above equation, we have

T_{new}=\frac{\rho_{old}}{\rho_{new}}\times T_{old}

Constant Draft
Sometimes, there is a need to ballast the ship because the draft of the ship has to remain unchanged when it travels from waters of different mass density.

The new displacement of the ship can be calculated from

\nabla_{new}=\frac{\rho_{new}}{\rho_{old}} \times \nabla_{old}

TPC
TPC is known as the tonnes per 1cm immersion.

It is the measurement of the mass that must be loaded or discharged to change the ship's mean draft by 1cm.

Unit of TPC is given as tonnes/cm.

The TPC is given as

TPC_{salt-water}=\frac{WPA}{100} \times \rho_{salt-water}

Displacement Curve
In ship design, a displacement curve is used to record the displacement of the ship for a series of draft.

Such curve is used to find the displacement of the ship for any particular draft.

The displacement curve consists of a draft scale plotted on the vertical axis and the displacement scale plotted on the horizontal axis.
