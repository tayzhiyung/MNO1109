For the analysis for combined list and trim, the two parts must be treated separately.

It is usually more convenient to starts with the trim part and followed by the list.

General

When a ship such as tanker and LNG is carrying liquid, it is subjected to movement of liquid known as sloshing.

As a results, additional moment known as free surface moment from the surface of the liquid is induced on to the ship, thus reducing the stability of the ship.

Such effect is known as the free surface effect, FSE.



×
×

Reducing Free Surface Moment
The FSE will reduce the GM of the ship.

There are several ways to reduce the FSE, and one of the common methods is to introduce longitudinal divisional bulkheads.

The divisional bulkhead is able to reduce the virtual loss of GM, which is calculated by,

GM_{virtual loss}=\frac{i}{W} \times \rho \times \frac{1}{n^2} [meters]
< where
i is the second moment of the free surface about the centerline [m4]
W is the ship's displacement [tonnes]
ρ is the mass density of the liquid in the tank [tonnes/cubic meter]
n is the number of longitudinal compartments into which the tank is equally subdivided

General(1)

If a compartment in the ship below the waterline is holed (damaged) such to an extent that water flow freely into and out of the compartment, the vessel is said to be bilged.



×
×

General(2)
When the ship is bilged, the buoyancy provided by the bilged compartment is lost.

The draft has increased and the vessel now floats at waterline W1L1.

The increase of draft is given as ,

T_{increase}=\frac{V_{lost}}{WPA_{intact}}


T_{increase}=\frac{V}{A-a}
where,
Tincrease is the amount of increased draft
Vlost is the lost of volume V
WPAintact is the intact waterplane area
A is the WPA before bilging
a is the area of bilge compartment

Note 1: Since water at the bilge area is free to move in and out, thus there is no change in the distribution of weight within the vessel. Therefore the KG after bilging will be the same as the KG before bilging.

Note 2: The draft increases because due to the lost of buoyancy, the ship needs to displace more water to make up the buoyancy lost.
