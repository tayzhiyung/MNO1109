General
In ship's design, due to its irregular shape, the use of approximate integration or numerical integration is needed to find certain properties of the ship.

The most common approximate integration is the Simpson's rule, and it is usually used to find the area, volume, centre of floatation, centre of buoyancy and second moment of inertia of the ship.

It is to be noted that Simpson's rule only gives an approximate value to these properties and hence it is important to choose the integration interval wisely.

There are three types of Simpson's rule, i.e. Simpson's 1st Rule, Simpson's 2nd Rule and Simpson's 3rd Rule.

Simpson's 1st Rule

In the Simpson's 1st Rule, the curve is assumed to be parabolic of the second order and must be continuous.

Waterplane Area
The waterplane area is defined as the surface area confined within the ship at its free surface.

The waterplane area can be obtained by using Simpson's rule by treating each half-ordinate as y-variable in the Simpson's rule.



×
×

Cross Sectional Area
The cross sectional area is defined as the transverse surface area confined within the ship at each station.

The cross sectional area is important to obtain the underwater volume of the ship.



×
×

Underwater Volume
The underwater volume of the ship is the volume of the ship being displaced when floating in equilibrium.

The underwater volume of the ship can be obtained by using Simpson's rule by treating the waterplane area or the cross sectional area as the y-variable in the Simpson's rule.

Center of Flotation

The centre of flotation is defined as the centre of the waterplane area and can be obtained from the first moment of the waterplane area.

This can also be done by using Simpson's rule. The CoF can be described in the x and y-axis.

The y-component of the CoF is usually located at the centreline of a symmetrical ship. The x-component of the CoF is always referred to the midship.

If it is located to the right of the midship (more towards the bow), it is stated as 'forward midship'; if it is located to the left (toward the stern), it is stated as 'aft midship'.
