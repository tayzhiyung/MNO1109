What is List?
When there is an uneven distribution of weight in the transverse direction, the ship lists (rotate).

Sometimes, from collision or bad weather, one side of the ship is damaged and water enters compartments in that side, then the ship lists to that side.Â 

The ship listing (heeling) will continue until it attains equilibrium, i.e. the CoB and the CoG are in the same vertical line.

Moment
When the weight already on board the ship is shiftted transversely (from left to right), the centre of gravity G will move to G' in the same direction parallel to the path of the shifted weight.

This will produced a listing moment W x GG1, where W is the displacement of the ship. The ship will list until G1 and the CoB are in the same vertical line.

Final position of CoG is found by taking moments about the keel and about the centreline.

Final List
The final list of the ship can be obtained by using trigonometry.

When the ship is in equilibrium, G1 and B will lie vertically under M so long as the angle of list is small.

Therefore it forms a triangle GG1M (which is right-angled of G), and if M and G are known, the final list can be found.

Heel
List is not to be confused with heel. Trim is therotation of the ship due to external loading such as wind.

When the external loading is removed, the ship will return to its initial position.

Heel is temporary.

List and Heel should also not be confused with Trim, which will be taught in the next section

KG
There are three componnents for the centre of gravity of a ship, i.e. the x, y and z direction.

The y-component of the CoG for a barehull is located at the centreline for a symmetrical ship.

The CoG for the x- and z-direction can be obtained from Simpson's rule as taught in Chapter 2.

The CoG in the x-direction is reference with respect to the midship whereas the z-component of the CoG is usually reference to the ship keel and terms KG.
