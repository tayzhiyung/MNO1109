Moment
The statical stability of a ship is different when it is inclined at a large angle.

At a large angle of heel, the force of buoyancy is no longer acting vertically upwards through the initial metacenter (M).

In such case, the CoB has moved further out to the low side, and the vertical through B1, no longer passes through M.

Remember that the righting lever GZ is the perpendicular distance between the vertical line of forces through G and B1, and therefore the moment of statical stability is equal to GM sin(θ).

However, GZ is no longer equal to GM sin(θ). Up to the angle at which the deck edge is immersed, it may be found by using a formula known as the wall sided-formula, i.e.

GZ=(GM+\frac{1}{2} BM tan^2 \theta) sin(\theta)

GZ Cross Curves of Stability

GZ cross curves of stability are set of curves from which the righting lever about an assumed CoG for any angle of heel at any particular displacement maybe found by inspection.

If the actual KG of the ship differs from this, a correction must be applied to the righting levers taken from the curves.

KN Cross Curves of Stability

The stabilty cross curves for ship are constructed by plotting the righting lever for an assumed height of the CoG above the keel.

The curves are referred to as KN curves.
KN Curves are used as it is independent of the CoG.

The GZ obtained from KN curve is given as,

GZ = KN - KG sin(\theta)
