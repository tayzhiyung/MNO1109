General Notes for Linesplan

In ship design, the drawing of ship is represented by Linesplan.

Linesplan allows the representation of ship, which is curvy in geometry, by using the projection of the intersection of the hull with a series of planes.

Three planes are usually used i.e. the top, side and front view (planes). The planes are with equally spaced in each of the three dimensions. These set of planes are mutually perpendicular or orthogonal in nature.

×
×

Description of Linesplan

The projection of ships can be made to three different planes, and resulted in three different views (projections), called the Body Plan, Half-Breadth Plan and Sheer (Profile) Plan.

For each projection, the ship is represented by
1. station
2. buttock line
3. waterline

Stations are equal interval spacing along the length of the ship, that starts from the After Perpendicular (AP) to the Forward Perpendicular (FP) .

Buttock line represents curve lines that are formed from intersection between the hull form and the longitudinal plane.

Waterlines are curve lines formed from intersection between ship hull and waterplane.

It is important to be able to visualise the difference between waterplane, transverse plane and longitudinal plane.

Equilibrium

Principle of Archimedes states that the upward buoyant force that is exerted on a body immersed in a fluid, whether fully or partially submerged, is equal to the weight of the fluid that the body displaces and acts in the upward direction at the center of mass of the displaced fluid.

The buoyancy force is measured as the weight of the displaced volume.

The ship will sink if the weight is greater than the buoyancy and will rise when the buoyancy is greater than the weight.

When the weight is equal to the buoyancy, the ship is said to be in equilibrium position.

Tonnage
Tonnage is used to measure the size and load carrying capacity of the ship.

It is a volume measurement and not a weight measurement.

One standard ton = 100 cubic feet.

There are two different tonnages, i.e.
1. gross tonnage - represent overall size of a ship and use to measure the difficulty of handling and berthing,
2. Net tonnage - represent its carrying capacity and used to measure the earning ability of the ship

Mass Density
Mass density ρ is defined as the amount of mass M contained per unit volume V.

\rho=\frac{M}{V}

Unit for mass density is kg/m3

×
×

Specific Gravity
Specific gravity SG is the ratio of mass density ρ to a reference mass density ρ0, usually taken as the mass density of water, i.e. 1000kg/m3.

SG=\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}

TPC
TPC is known as the tonnes per 1cm immersion.

It is the measurement of the mass that must be loaded or discharged to change the ship's mean draft by 1cm.

Unit of TPC is given as tonnes/cm.

The TPC is given as

TPC_{salt-water}=\frac{WPA}{100} \times \rho_{salt-water}

The displacement curve consists of a draft scale plotted on the vertical axis and the displacement scale plotted on the horizontal axis.
